Nexus: A Brief History of Information Networks from the Stone Age to AI

From the author of Sapiens comes the groundbreaking story of how information networks have made, and unmade, our world.

Book cover. Source: https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/204927599-nexus

About this book:

  • Author: Yuval Noah Harari
  • #1 New York Times bestseller
  • Publisher: Random House Publishing Group
  • Publication date: 09/10/2024
  • Pages: 528
  • Key themes:
    • Information as a fundamental building block of reality: Harari argues that information is the glue that holds human societies together, and its flow has been instrumental in shaping our world.
    • The Stone Age to the Bible: Harari examines how early human societies used information networks, such as oral traditions and written texts, to establish social hierarchies, myths, and legends.
    • Witch-hunts and the rise of bureaucracy: The author discusses how the Protestant Reformation and the Enlightenment led to the development of more complex information networks, including printing presses and bureaucracies, which were used to spread ideas and impose order.
    • Stalinism and Nazism: Harari analyzes how totalitarian regimes used information networks to manipulate and control populations, creating mass delusions and fictions.
    • The digital age and AI: The book explores the implications of AI on our information networks, highlighting both the potential benefits (e.g., increased efficiency, access to knowledge) and risks (e.g., manipulation, misinformation).
    • The importance of critical thinking and human agency: Harari emphasizes the need for humans to understand and navigate the complexities of information networks, rather than being controlled by them.
